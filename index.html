Talking about the whole package which includes solutions that work together, let's see an example on how 3 different project-ideas will solve one of New York's most vexing problems, the delivery.

Let’s start with the problem.

As you can see on the picture, The New York Times and so many media leaders have reported about this issue. Streets are being used as storage spots, delivery trucks such as UPS, FeDex or USPS double-park on streets and block bus and bike lanes. In addition to the palettes you see around grocery supermarkets like Trader Joe's, Gristedes or Whole Foods, 18-wheeled trucks or less delivering pallets in front of the stores, which creates a sequence of events that obstruct traffic, pedestrians, bikes … etc. Unquestionably an everyday delivery causes a mess, which costs $ billions yearly in productivity lost - as a direct result.

What departments are concerned about nailing down the delivery problem?

NYC Department Of Transportation & NYPD Traffic.

Solutions:

Solution #1: City-Hydrant

Re-inventing fire-hydrant to fit first safety, then the economy second. 

There are almost 3200 fire-hydrants in only Manhattan borough, to smartly use these thousands of temporary-parking spots - will generate taxes to the city, much more than the current stupid way of generating a couple $ millions yearly - as an outcome of settleing parking tickets with big compnaies like UPS and FeDex. 

The spots may also used for NYPD officers or other city workers who may eligible for such a parking by the futuristic Ally's Law Algorithm.  

Solution #2: City-Starter

In case of an emergency, first-responders will be able to get an instant pin - to start delivery trucks and move them away.

As we mentioned before at solution #1 - safety comes first.
City-Hydrant designed for the opposite side of a street or an avenue, but in some emergency cases, first-responders will need to move the vehicle.
At a glance, how does it work?
+ It’s an emergency and the delivery-man isn’t on site?
+ Radio-call your base, get a pin quickly, start the engine, move the delivery truck away.
"Supervisors may have a special fob that starts delivery trucks engines immediately. It’s up to New York’s upcoming elected-officials, on how they’re aiming to regulate such a bill"

Solution #3: City-Delivery

Night-time and Off-peak Secured Pallets for freight and other delivery workers.

It's like an 'Amazon Locker' for freight pallets! It also works as a secured storage cart, which all kinds of delivery workers use it, of course with very strict instructions that will be provided by our futuristic algorithm, we also call it Ally’s Law.
How does it work with freight delivery?
+ Log into DOT-freight App, which will be connected to the Ally’s Law Algorithm.
+ See what time you’re allowed to deliver.
+ Head to the App-provided pallet location.
+ Put the pallet in, and lock it.
+ During daytime, grocery stores send their workers to get the pallet.
